# Phase 07.2 — Per-Channel Thompson Sampling + Office-Scoped Candidates

## Problem

After phase 07.1 shipped multi-office support, two real bugs surfaced on the live deploy:

1. **G-01 — Polls pull from every office.** A channel bound to office A gets restaurants from office B because `get_candidate_pool` filters only on `workspace_id`. The `restaurants` table has no `location_id`.
2. **G-02 — Stats are shared across channels.** `restaurant_stats` is keyed at the workspace (or workspace+restaurant) level, so two channels bound to the same office contaminate each other's Thompson sampling priors. One team's taste leaks into another team's poll.

## Locked decisions

- **Candidate pool is office-scoped.** Restaurants carry a `location_id` FK → `workspace_locations.id`. A poll in channel C uses the `location_id` from `channel_locations` for C.
- **Thompson sampling is per-channel.** `restaurant_stats` becomes keyed by `(channel_id, restaurant_id)`. Not per-office. Not per-workspace. Rationale: each channel is a distinct team; their votes should not pollute another team's posteriors even when they share an office.

## Scope

1. **Migration 009** — add `restaurants.location_id` FK (nullable initially), backfill existing rows to each workspace's default office, then set NOT NULL. Re-key `restaurant_stats` on `channel_id` (either wipe + re-accumulate, or split existing rows evenly — open question for planning).
2. **Seed path updates** — `lunchbot/blueprints/setup.py` install form and `lunchbot/blueprints/slack_actions.py` add-office modal submission must tag seeded restaurants with the new office's `location_id`.
3. **Query rewrite** — `lunchbot/client/db_client.py` `get_candidate_pool(poll_date, channel_id)` now:
   - resolves channel → `location_id` via `channel_locations`
   - filters `r.location_id = :location_id`
   - joins `restaurant_stats` on `(channel_id=:channel_id, restaurant_id=r.id)`
4. **Stats-processing job** (phase 4 reputation tracker) — emit per-channel `restaurant_stats` rows on poll finalize.
5. **Regression tests** — two offices, two channels bound to different offices, verify candidate pools do not overlap; two channels bound to the SAME office, verify their stats diverge independently.

## Open questions for planning

- Backfill strategy for existing `restaurant_stats`: wipe to uninformative prior `(1.0, 1.0)` vs. clone current row per existing channel? Wipe is simpler but loses history; clone preserves history but over-weights early channels.
- Should a deleted office cascade-delete its restaurants, or leave them orphaned pending reassignment?
- Does Phase 4's scheduled reputation job need a retroactive replay against `polls` history to populate per-channel stats?
